Cell Simulation
The cell simulation is a program that simulates a colony of cells growth and deaths. It compares each cell with their neighbors to determine if the cell will die or be alive in the next step, doing this to every cell for 100 steps.This program makes use of multiprocessing to handle the arrays, and it was tested with 10,000 x 10,000 array size, only taking less than 20 minutes with 36 processes.
Rules

Each cell has two states:
  O : Alive
  . : Dead

A cell's state in the next step will be determined by its neighbors and it's current state:

  • If the cell is alive and has 2 - 4 alive neighbors, it will stay alive in the next step.
  • If the cell is dead and there is an even number >0 of alive neighbors, it will become alive in the next step.
  • Otherwise, the cell becomes or stays dead.

A cell's neighbors are as follows:
   _ _ _ _ _   C N _ _ N
   _ N N N _   N N _ _ N
   _ N C N _   _ _ _ _ _
   _ N N N _   _ _ _ _ _
   _ _ _ _ _   N N _ _ N
